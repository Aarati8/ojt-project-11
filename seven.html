Overview

The Class 7 curriculum builds upon the foundational skills acquired in Class 6, introducing more complex concepts and encouraging deeper analytical thinking. In Language Arts, students transition from basic writing exercises to structured essays, debates, and text analysis. Emphasis is placed on persuasive writing, advanced grammar, and public speaking, equipping students with the communication skills needed for academic and real-life scenarios. Reading assignments include short stories, poems, and informational texts to broaden their literary exposure.

Mathematics in Class 7 advances to algebraic equations, statistics, and data interpretation. Students learn to solve word problems, create graphs, and understand patterns, which are essential for logical reasoning. Practical applications, such as calculating percentages or analyzing survey data, help students see the relevance of math in daily life. The Science curriculum delves into physics and chemistry basics, covering topics like force, energy, and chemical reactions. Hands-on experiments and lab activities make abstract concepts tangible, fostering a love for scientific exploration.

Social Studies expands to include global geography, civics, and economics. Students examine different political systems, cultural practices, and economic principles, encouraging a broader worldview. Discussions on Nepal’s constitutional framework and governance structures help them understand their rights and responsibilities as citizens. Life Skills education evolves to include leadership training, conflict resolution, and ethical decision-making. Role-playing activities and group projects teach students how to collaborate effectively and handle disagreements constructively.

The Arts program in Class 7 becomes more specialized, offering opportunities in drama, advanced visual arts, or music theory. Students may participate in school plays, art exhibitions, or musical performances, showcasing their talents and building self-confidence. Physical education continues to promote fitness and teamwork, with activities tailored to enhance coordination and endurance. These co-curricular components ensure that students develop holistically, balancing academic rigor with creative and physical growth.

Class 7 also introduces research-based projects, where students investigate topics of interest and present their findings. This fosters independent learning, critical analysis, and effective presentation skills. Teachers guide students in sourcing reliable information, organizing their work, and delivering clear, concise reports. By the end of the year, students are expected to demonstrate greater self-discipline, initiative, and the ability to think critically about complex issues.

In summary, the Class 7 curriculum challenges students to think deeper, communicate effectively, and engage with the world around them. It prepares them for the increased demands of higher grades while nurturing their intellectual, social, and creative potential.

Academic Tracks

Shivapuri Secondary School offers the CDC curriculum for Class 7 in Nepal covers a range of subjects including Nepali, English, Mathematics, Science and Technology, Social Studies, and Health and Physical Education. The curriculum emphasizes developing fundamental literacy and life skills, alongside fostering interest in arts and aesthetic values. It is designed to be relevant, practical, and contribute to the overall development of students.

Cirriculum

Class 7 builds on foundational skills with advanced grammar, algebra, physics/chemistry basics, and civic education. The schedule includes project-based learning and group discussions to enhance analytical abilities.

Based on the above context, the following competencies have been determined for early childhood development and education:

(A) Competence

  • Language: Write persuasive essays and analyze texts.
  • Mathematics: Solve algebraic equations and interpret data.
  • Science: Explain force, energy, and chemical reactions.
  • Social Studies: Compare global cultures and governance systems.
  • Life Skills: Lead team projects and resolve conflicts.
  • Arts: Perform in dramas or design creative projects.
