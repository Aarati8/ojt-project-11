Overview

The curriculum for Class 1 at Shivapuri Secondary School is designed to foster holistic development through play-based and interactive learning. At this foundational stage, children are introduced to basic literacy in Nepali and English, focusing on phonetic awareness, letter recognition, and simple vocabulary. Mathematics lessons revolve around counting (1-50), shapes, and practical arithmetic, using visual aids like blocks and pictures to make abstract concepts tangible.

Science and Environment studies encourage curiosity about the natural world, covering topics like body parts, plants, and animals through storytelling and hands-on activities. Social Studies introduces students to their immediate surroundings—family, school, and local festivals—to build cultural awareness and social skills. Creative Arts and Physical Education are integral, helping children develop fine motor skills through drawing, clay modeling, and group games while promoting teamwork and discipline.

The daily schedule balances structured lessons with recreational activities, ensuring young learners remain engaged without feeling overwhelmed. Competencies focus on observable outcomes like reciting alphabets, counting objects, and demonstrating basic hygiene—all aligned with CDC’s emphasis on learning through experience. Teachers use rhymes, puzzles, and role-play to create a joyful classroom environment that nurtures confidence and a love for learning.

Parental involvement is encouraged through activities like storytelling sessions and craft exhibitions, bridging the gap between school and home. By the end of Class 1, students are expected to communicate in simple sentences, follow classroom routines, and show curiosity about their environment—a strong foundation for higher grades.

Academic Tracks

The Curriculum Development Centre (CDC) in Nepal has developed an integrated curriculum for Grade 1, focusing on foundational skills and communicative competence in English. This curriculum emphasizes a communicative approach to language teaching, incorporating four language skills and prioritizing language functions. It is designed to be relevant, practical, and competitive, incorporating social values, globalization, and aspects of science and technology.

Cirriculum

Class 1 focuses on play-based learning to develop foundational literacy (Nepali/English) and numeracy skills. Children explore their environment through stories, songs, and hands-on activities. The curriculum emphasizes socialization, motor skills (writing, cutting), and curiosity about nature.

Based on the above context, the following competencies have been determined for early childhood development and education:

(A) Competence

  • Recite Nepali vowels/consonants.
  • Count objects up to 50.
  • Name common animals/plants.
  • Participate in group activities.
