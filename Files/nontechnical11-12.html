Overview

Stream Specialization
Science students focus on NEB Physics (optics experiments), Chemistry (organic synthesis), and Biology (microscopy). Management covers financial accounting (Trial Balance preparation) and business law.

Research Components
Science: Investigatory projects (testing natural pH indicators)
Humanities: Ethnographic studies of Nepali indigenous communities
Management: Market surveys of Kathmandu consumer trends

University Entrance Prep
NEET/CMAT question banks for Science
Case study analyses for Management
IELTS/TOEFL coaching for abroad aspirants

Community Engagement
Mandatory Social Internships (teaching at local primary schools, NGO fieldwork) develop civic responsibility. Students document 100+ service hours.

NEB Examination Focus
Strict adherence to board patterns:
75% theory (long-form answers)
25% practicals (viva voce, lab notebooks)
Model tests under timed conditions

Academic Tracks

The Curriculum Development Center (CDC) in Nepal has moved towards a single-track curriculum for grades 11 and 12, emphasizing skill-based subjects alongside core subjects. Previously, students chose between science, management, humanities, law, and education streams, but now English, Nepali, Social Studies (in Grade 11), and Life Skills (in Grade 12) are compulsory, with optional subjects focused on skill development.


Core Domains

Computer Science

  • Management: Accountancy, Business Studies.
  • Humanities: Sociology, Environment Science.

Key Focus Area

  • Research projects (e.g., case studies in Management).
  • University entrance prep (e.g., NEET for Science).

Cirriculum

Students choose specialized tracks, with Science stream focusing on advanced Physics/Chemistry labs, while Management covers financial accounting. The curriculum integrates life skills (e.g., entrepreneurship in Business Studies) and aligns with NEB board exams
