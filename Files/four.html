Overview

The Class 4 curriculum under Nepal’s National Curriculum Framework (NCF) 2076 is designed to strengthen foundational skills while fostering curiosity and creativity. The language component focuses on Nepali and English literacy, emphasizing reading fluency, basic grammar (nouns, verbs, tenses), and simple writing tasks like descriptive paragraphs and letters. Lessons often integrate local folktales and cultural stories to make learning relatable. Oral communication is nurtured through storytelling, recitations, and group discussions, ensuring students develop confidence in expressing their ideas.

In mathematics, students transition from basic arithmetic to more structured problem-solving. They work with 3-4 digit numbers, mastering addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division. Practical applications, such as measuring lengths, weights, and volumes, help them understand real-world uses of math. Geometry introduces 2D and 3D shapes, symmetry, and spatial reasoning through hands-on activities like constructing models. Word problems are framed around local contexts, such as market transactions or farming yields, to enhance engagement.

Science in Class 4 is exploratory, covering life sciences (plants, animals, ecosystems), physical sciences (states of matter, energy), and basic earth science (weather, water cycle). Simple experiments, like observing plant growth or testing materials for buoyancy, encourage scientific inquiry and observation skills. The curriculum connects science to environmental awareness, teaching students about conservation, recycling, and sustainable practices in their communities.

Social studies bridges local and national identity, introducing students to Nepal’s geography (rivers, mountains, cities), history (key figures, cultural heritage), and civic responsibilities. Lessons on community roles, festivals, and traditions foster a sense of belonging. Interactive methods, such as map-drawing, role-playing village governance, and interviewing elders about local history, make learning dynamic and participatory.

Life skills and values are a critical part of Class 4, addressing personal hygiene, teamwork, conflict resolution, and emotional well-being. Activities like group projects, peer discussions, and role-playing scenarios help students develop empathy and cooperation. Creative arts—drawing, painting, music, and drama—are integrated weekly, allowing students to express themselves while learning about Nepal’s cultural arts.

The assessment approach is formative and competency-based, using quizzes, oral presentations, project work, and teacher observations to track progress. The focus is on continuous improvement rather than high-pressure exams, ensuring a stress-free and engaging learning environment for young students.

Academic Tracks

Shivapuri Secondary School offers the CDC (Curriculum Development Centre) curriculum for Grade 4 focuses on developing fundamental literacy and life skills, alongside fostering an interest in arts and aesthetics. The curriculum aims to build a strong foundation for students in English, Mathematics, Science & Technology, Social Studies, and Health, Physical & Creative Arts. It emphasizes practical application, critical thinking, and preparing students for further education and life.


1. Language and Communication

  • Reading: Fluency, comprehension of short stories, poems, and informational texts.
  • Writing: Structured paragraphs, creative writing (stories, letters), and basic grammar (nouns, verbs, adjectives).
  • Listening & Speaking: Following instructions, storytelling, and simple presentations.
  • Vocabulary: Context-based word meanings, synonyms, and antonyms.

2. Mathematics

  • Number Sense: Four operations (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division) with 3–4 digit numbers.
  • Fractions & Decimals: Basic introduction (halves, quarters, tenths).
  • Geometry: 2D/3D shapes, symmetry, and simple measurements (length, weight).
  • Problem-Solving: Word problems involving real-life scenarios.

3. Science

  • Life Science: Plant/animal life cycles, habitats, and adaptations.
  • Physical Science: States of matter, simple machines, and energy (light, sound).
  • Earth Science: Weather patterns, water cycle, and natural resources.
  • Scientific Method: Basic experiments and observations (e.g., plant growth).

4. Social Studies

  • History: Local traditions, folktales, and national heroes.
  • Geography: Maps (key features, directions), landforms (mountains, rivers).
  • Civics: Community roles, basic rights/responsibilities.
  • Culture: Festivals, food, and clothing across regions.

5. Life Skills & Values

  • Personal Development: Hygiene, time management, and goal-setting.
  • Social Skills: Teamwork, conflict resolution, and empathy.
  • Health: Nutrition, exercise, and safety (road, fire).
  • Environment: Recycling, conservation, and caring for nature.

6. Creative Arts

  • Visual Arts: Drawing, painting, and crafts (using recyclable materials).
  • Performing Arts: Folk songs, role-playing, and simple dances.

Cirriculum

The Class 4 curriculum focuses on building foundational skills across core subjects while nurturing curiosity and creativity. In language studies, students develop basic literacy through reading simple texts, writing short paragraphs, and participating in oral communication activities. Mathematics emphasizes practical arithmetic operations and introductory geometry. Science explores basic concepts through hands-on experiments with plants, materials, and weather patterns. Social studies introduces local geography, community roles, and cultural traditions. The curriculum also incorporates essential life skills like personal hygiene and teamwork, along with creative arts activities that connect to Nepal's cultural heritage. Assessment is competency-based, using observation and simple projects to track progress in a stress-free learning environment.

Based on the above context, the following competencies have been determined for early childhood development and education:

(A) Competence

Language (Nepali/English)

  • Read and comprehend grade-level texts with fluency
  • Write simple paragraphs (5–8 sentences) with proper grammar
  • Participate in group discussions and storytelling
  • Use basic vocabulary in context (synonyms, antonyms)

Mathematics

  • Perform addition/subtraction of 4-digit numbers
  • Solve simple multiplication (2-digit × 1-digit) and division problems
  • Measure length/weight using standard units (meter, gram)
  • Identify 2D/3D shapes and symmetry

Science

  • Classify local plants/animals and their habitats
  • Conduct basic experiments (e.g., floating/sinking objects)
  • Record weather observations (temperature, rainfall)

Social Studies

  • Map local community features (rivers, roads, landmarks)
  • Explain cultural festivals and traditions
  • Describe roles of community helpers (farmers, teachers)

Life Skills

  • Practice personal hygiene and time management
  • Resolve conflicts through role-playing
  • Participate in environmental conservation (recycling)

Creative Arts

  • Create art using local materials (clay, recycled items)
  • Perform folk songs/dances

Assessment:

  • Oral reading fluency checks
  • Math manipulative activities
  • Science observation journals
