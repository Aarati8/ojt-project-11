General Information

Shivapuri Secondary School, established in 1948 A.D. (2005 B.S.), is a model public school of Maharajgunj, Kathmandu, that stands as a pillar of Nepal's educational hub. Having a history of over 75 years, it has ever pursued its vision of providing equal, quality education to students belonging to different socio-economic backgrounds.

It has school-level affiliations with the National Examinations Board (NEB) and undergraduate affiliations with Tribhuvan University (TU). The school is currently enrolling more than 1,350 students in its rolls and is served by an extremely dedicated 65-member faculty specialized in various fields.

The response unfolds in a campus setting that mirrors across multiple buildings equipped with high-tech classrooms, digital teaching boards, high-speed internet connectivity, a library of more than 10,000 physical and electronic books, and multipurpose auditoria for academic and extracurricular activities. Shivapuri's educational approach is a blend of intensive academic preparation with values-based education focusing on discipline, compassion, and citizenship.

Academic Offerings

School Level (NEB):

+2 Management: The curriculum is designed to provide students with general and applied knowledge in fields such as accounting, business economics, marketing, and human resource management. Special seminars, internships, and field work are integrated into train students for real challenges.

Technical Stream – Computer Engineering (Grades 9–12):

Established in response to Nepal's growing demand for technically competent youth, this stream is equivalent to a Diploma in Computer Engineering. Courses consist of structured programming, computer hardware maintenance and networking, and software design. Entrance is based on merit and personal motivation to encourage academic excellence and career focus.

College Level (TU):

Bachelor of Business Studies (BBS): 4-year undergraduate degree specializing in business strategy, entrepreneurship, managerial economics, and business research. Analytical reasoning, teamwork, leadership, and decision-making are the cornerstone of the curriculum. Its graduates secure over 97% employment or higher study placement within six months of graduation.

Core Values & Digital Integration

Shivapuri is committed to all-round development so that students not only graduate with degrees but also with a well-rounded worldview about life. Its embracing of digital classrooms, smart boards, virtual labs, and hybrid models of learning has put it on the cutting edge of public school education. Regular updation of the IT infrastructure ensures teachers and students remain digitally equipped and globally competitive.

The institution has been formally granted the title of a model school by Kathmandu Metropolitan City in light of its success in pursuing technical and computer-based learning at the level of community schools.

Community Impact

Aside from education, Shivapuri also conducts regular community outreach activities. Some of its achievements include:

  • HPV vaccination drive, launched with the collaboration of the Ministry of Health and inaugurated by Prime Minister KP Sharma Oli, focused on public health for adolescent girls.
  • Participation in the President's Education Reform Programme, where students participate in policy input design.
  • Hosting annual environmental awareness weeks, tree-planting drives, and national development public debate sessions.

History

Founding & Early Challenges (1948–2010)

Shivapuri's history is firmly rooted in Nepal's post-Rana democratic renaissance. It was established as a neighborhood community school by local educators, social reformers, and philanthropists and emerged as a symbol of educational transformation at a time when access to schools was strictly restricted, especially for marginalized sections of society.

In its early years, the school operated in rented buildings using volunteer teachers and limited infrastructure. Despite adversity, including opposition to formal schooling and lack of infrastructure, the school grew incrementally driven by community commitment and sponsorship from grassroots organizations.

The initial attempts at initiating a technical stream were brought about by the approval of the curriculum, teachers' availability, and equipment purchase. Despite this, through collaborations with Kathmandu Metropolitan City and donor support schemes from abroad, Shivapuri was laying the groundwork for further growth in future years.

Growth & Modernization (2010–Present)

Shivapuri established Shivapuri College in 2010 due to alumni proposals and growing demand for access to higher education within the area, initiating the TU-affiliated BBS program. It was a transition from being a school to a composite institution offering secondary and tertiary education under one umbrella.

Shivapuri, in 2015, became one of the very first community schools in Nepal to introduce a Computer Engineering stream in Grades 9–12. The curriculum for the technical streams was developed in consultation with IT professionals and TU advisors to make it relevant and challenging.

In the 2020s, Shivapuri made expansion plans to include new schools of Hotel Management, Information Technology, and Tourism that align with Nepal's emerging sectors of the economy. It also invested in e-learning modules, and smart classrooms and digital attendance systems to modernize administrative efficiency.

Alumni Legacy

Shivapuri boasts an illustrious legacy of alumni who have left their mark on numerous sectors. Some prominent alumni include:

  • Founders of software companies and tech startups

Current Administration

Campus Chief: Dibakar Durdarshi, appointed in 2021, is an advocate for skills-based, career-oriented education and has introduced many capacity-building initiatives for teachers and students.

CMC Chairman: Jeet Bahadur Thapa oversees infrastructure rejuvenation, public partnerships, and donor engagement.

Faculty & Staff

The college consists of 65+ teaching faculty, all of whom are well-qualified in their area.

Faculty are regularly trained in:

  • Digital pedagogy
  • Inclusive classroom management
  • Research methodology
  • Student mental health and counseling

Staff are selected through competitive processes, and a few are active contributors to national academic journals and curriculum development committees.

Governance Model

Shivapuri has a blended public-community model of governance. The key features are:

  • College Management Committee (CMC) consisting of faculty, local government representatives, and community leaders
  • School Management Committee (SMC) with parents' representatives to ensure transparency
  • Regular academic audits and student feedback surveys

The structure focuses on accountability, teamwork, and representation, so that a balanced governing body can ensure effective management.

Formal Presidents

Shivapuri Secondary School has been guided by several visionary leaders whose contributions helped shape its values, infrastructure, and progressive educational environment.

Each president has brought unique perspectives and initiatives that have contributed to the school's growth and reputation in the educational community.

Their leadership has been instrumental in establishing the school as a respected institution in Nepal's educational landscape.

Facilities

Academic Infrastructure

Shivapuri infrastructure is specially designed with 21st-century education in mind:

Computer Lab: More than 50 networked computers with broadband internet connectivity pre-installed with programming and design software.
Science Laboratories: Dedicated labs for Physics, Chemistry, and Biology equipped with modern tools, safety systems, and demonstration facilities.
Library: A well-lit, air-cooled facility with 10,000+ titles, multimedia stations, and subscriptions to national and international journals.
Multipurpose Auditorium: Equipped with projectors, sound systems, and seating for 300+ participants.

Extracurricular Activities

Shivapuri emphasizes balanced growth through diverse activities:

Robotics Club: Builds autonomous bots and represents the school at national tech expos.
Literary and Debate Society: Conducts inter-school debates, poetry recitals, and publication drives.
Shivapuri Mahotsav: A festive annual celebration with music, cultural performances, and exhibitions.
Athletics & Sports: Inter-house football, basketball, and martial arts sports.

Student Support Services

Cafeteria: Balanced food at subsidized prices.
Counseling Unit: Qualified psychologists to assist in emotional well-being.
Health Center: Equipped for first aid and minor illnesses; works in conjunction with TU Teaching Hospital for referrals.
Career Cell: Provides resume-building workshops, internships, and alumni mentorship.
