Overview

The Class 5 curriculum builds on foundational skills while introducing more complex, analytical, and application-based learning. In language, students advance to critical reading, essay writing, and structured debates. They analyze different text genres‚Äîmyths, biographies, news articles‚Äîand practice persuasive and narrative writing. Grammar lessons cover tenses, prepositions, and sentence structures, with an emphasis on clear and effective communication. Public speaking activities, such as speeches and group discussions, prepare students for leadership roles.

Mathematics in Class 5 shifts toward abstract reasoning and real-world problem-solving. Students work with larger numbers (up to millions), decimals, and percentages, applying them to scenarios like budgeting, data interpretation, and measurements. Geometry lessons explore angles, area, perimeter, and volume, often through practical tasks like designing floor plans. Logical puzzles and multi-step word problems enhance critical thinking and perseverance.

Science becomes more rigorous, covering human biology (digestive, respiratory systems), physics (forces, electricity), and earth science (solar system, rocks, climate zones). Students conduct hypothesis-driven experiments, such as testing conductivity of materials or tracking weather patterns, to develop scientific reasoning. Environmental education ties into global sustainability challenges, encouraging students to brainstorm local solutions.

Social studies expands to national and global perspectives, examining Nepal‚Äôs government structure, historical movements, and economic systems. Comparative studies of world geography, cultures, and civilizations broaden students‚Äô worldviews. Projects like mock elections, heritage preservation campaigns, and case studies on global issues make learning interactive and relevant.

Life skills in Class 5 address adolescent development, digital literacy, leadership, and mental health awareness. Students engage in peer mentoring, community service projects, and critical discussions on ethics and media influence. Creative arts encourage higher-level expression‚Äîscriptwriting, advanced crafts, and performances‚Äîlinking tradition with innovation.

Assessments are diverse and competency-focused, including research projects, presentations, peer reviews, and portfolios. Rubrics assess critical thinking, creativity, and collaboration, aligning with Nepal‚Äôs vision of holistic, future-ready education.

Academic Tracks

Shivapuri Secondary School offers the CDC (Curriculum Development Centre) in Nepal outlines a curriculum for Class 5 that aims to develop fundamental literacy and life skills in students, while also fostering an interest in arts and aesthetics. The curriculum is designed to be relevant, practical, qualitative, and useful for the overall development of the learners. It focuses on developing basic literacy skills, life skills, and aesthetic appreciation through various subjects.


  1. Language and Communication
    • Reading: Fluency, comprehension of short stories, poems, and informational texts.
    • Writing: Structured paragraphs, creative writing (stories, letters), and basic grammar (nouns, verbs, adjectives).
    • Listening & Speaking: Following instructions, storytelling, and simple presentations.
    • Vocabulary: Context-based word meanings, synonyms, and antonyms.
  2. Mathematics
    • Number Sense: Four operations (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division) with 3‚Äì4 digit numbers.
    • Fractions & Decimals: Basic introduction (halves, quarters, tenths).
    • Geometry: 2D/3D shapes, symmetry, and simple measurements (length, weight).
    • Problem-Solving: Word problems involving real-life scenarios.
  3. Science
    • Life Science: Plant/animal life cycles, habitats, and adaptations.
    • Physical Science: States of matter, simple machines, and energy (light, sound).
    • Earth Science: Weather patterns, water cycle, and natural resources.
    • Scientific Method: Basic experiments and observations (e.g., plant growth).
  4. Social Studies
    • History: Local traditions, folktales, and national heroes.
    • Geography: Maps (key features, directions), landforms (mountains, rivers).
    • Civics: Community roles, basic rights/responsibilities.
    • Culture: Festivals, food, and clothing across regions.
  5. Life Skills & Values
    • Personal Development: Hygiene, time management, and goal-setting.
    • Social Skills: Teamwork, conflict resolution, and empathy.
    • Health: Nutrition, exercise, and safety (road, fire).
    • Environment: Recycling, conservation, and caring for nature.
  6. Creative Arts
    • Visual Arts: Drawing, painting, and crafts (using recyclable materials).
    • Performing Arts: Folk songs, role-playing, and simple dances.

Cirriculum

The Class 5 curriculum advances to more complex, analytical learning while maintaining real-world relevance. Language studies shift to critical reading, structured essay writing, and formal debates. Mathematics introduces abstract concepts like percentages and geometry with practical applications in budgeting and measurement. Science delves into human biology, physics principles, and earth systems through hypothesis-driven experiments. Social studies expands to examine national governance structures and global connections. The curriculum emphasizes 21st-century skills including digital literacy, leadership, and problem-solving through project-based learning. Creative arts challenge students with advanced techniques while maintaining cultural connections. Assessment methods become more sophisticated, incorporating research projects, presentations, and rubrics to evaluate higher-order thinking skills.

Based on the above context, the following competencies have been determined for early childhood development and education:

(A) Competence

Language (Nepali/English)

  • Read and comprehend grade-level texts with fluency
  • Write simple paragraphs (5‚Äì8 sentences) with proper grammar
  • Participate in group discussions and storytelling
  • Use basic vocabulary in context (synonyms, antonyms)

Mathematics

  • Perform addition/subtraction of 4-digit numbers
  • Solve simple multiplication (2-digit √ó 1-digit) and division problems
  • Measure length/weight using standard units (meter, gram)
  • Identify 2D/3D shapes and symmetry

Science

  • Classify local plants/animals and their habitats
  • Conduct basic experiments (e.g., floating/sinking objects)
  • Record weather observations (temperature, rainfall)

Social Studies

  • Map local community features (rivers, roads, landmarks)
  • Explain cultural festivals and traditions
  • Describe roles of community helpers (farmers, teachers)

Life Skills

  • Practice personal hygiene and time management
  • Resolve conflicts through role-playing
  • Participate in environmental conservation (recycling)

Creative Arts

  • Create art using local materials (clay, recycled items)
  • Perform folk songs/dances

Assessment

  • Oral reading fluency checks
  • Math manipulative activities
  • Science observation journals
